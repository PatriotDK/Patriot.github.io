# EU AI Act Overview

## Purpose

The EU AI Act introduces a risk-based regulatory framework for artificial intelligence systems.

Its purpose is to promote trustworthy AI while protecting health, safety, and fundamental rights.

---

## Core Approach

The regulation follows a risk-based model where AI systems may be categorized as:

- unacceptable risk
- high risk
- limited risk
- minimal risk

Different obligations apply depending on the level of risk.

---

## Key Themes

The EU AI Act focuses on:

- safety and robustness
- transparency
- accountability
- human oversight
- documentation
- risk management

---

## High-Risk AI Systems

High-risk systems are subject to stricter requirements, including:

- risk management processes
- data governance measures
- technical documentation
- logging capabilities
- transparency obligations
- human oversight
- accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity requirements

---

## Practical Relevance

The Act is highly relevant for organizations that:

- develop AI systems
- deploy AI in decision-making
- procure third-party AI solutions
- integrate AI into regulated environments

---

## Key Takeaway

AI governance is not only about innovation. It is also about ensuring that AI systems are lawful, controlled, transparent, and accountable.
