# AI Risk Assessment

## Objective

An AI risk assessment helps identify and evaluate legal, technical, operational, and governance-related risks associated with an AI system.

---

## Core Questions

A structured AI risk assessment should ask:

- What is the purpose of the system?
- What decisions or outputs does it influence?
- What data does it use?
- Who may be affected?
- What are the possible harms?
- What controls exist?
- Who is accountable?

---

## Example Risk Categories

### Legal and Regulatory Risk
- non-compliance with AI regulation
- GDPR-related issues
- lack of lawful basis for data use

### Operational Risk
- inaccurate outputs
- over-reliance on automated results
- poor integration into workflows

### Governance Risk
- unclear accountability
- insufficient documentation
- lack of approval processes

### Security Risk
- model misuse
- data leakage
- adversarial manipulation
- insecure third-party providers

---

## Assessment Steps

1. Define use case
2. Identify stakeholders
3. Map data and system inputs
4. Assess potential harms
5. Review controls and safeguards
6. Evaluate residual risk
7. Document decisions and responsibilities

---

## Key Takeaway

AI risk assessment should not be treated as a one-time exercise. It should be part of ongoing governance, review, and accountability.
